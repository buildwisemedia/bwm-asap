Exclusion & Sealing — The Step Most Companies Skip

Trapping animals without sealing entry points is a temporary fix. We close every gap, crack, and weak spot so wildlife stays out for good. Warranty-backed.

The Problem

If You Don't Seal It, They're Coming Back

Most wildlife companies trap the animal and leave. That's only half the job. The holes are still there. The scent trail is still there. A new animal finds the same entry point — sometimes within days — and the cycle starts over.

Your home has dozens of potential entry points: soffit gaps, roof returns, pipe penetrations, gable vents, foundation cracks, and construction gaps you can't see from the ground. A rat only needs a hole the size of a quarter. A mouse can squeeze through a gap the width of a pencil.

Common entry points we seal:

  • 🛠 Soffit & fascia gaps
  • 🏠 Roof returns & ridge vents
  • 🔧 Pipe & wire penetrations
  • 💨 Gable vents
  • 🧱 Foundation cracks
  • 🚪 Garage & door gaps
  • 💧 Dryer & exhaust vents

Exclusion & Sealing FAQ

We use galvanized steel mesh, metal flashing, heavy-gauge hardware cloth, and professional-grade sealants. These materials are chosen to withstand chewing, weather, and time. We don't use foam or caulk alone — animals chew right through those.
