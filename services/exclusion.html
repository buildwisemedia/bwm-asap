How We Handle Exclusion Work

Step 1

Full Property Inspection

We inspect your entire roofline, foundation, soffit, vents, and every seam in your home. We document every gap with photos so you can see exactly what we find.

Step 2

Identify All Entry Points

We map every opening — even the ones you can't see from the ground. Roof returns, pipe penetrations, gable vents, soffit gaps. If a finger can fit through it, so can a mouse.

Step 3

Seal With Professional Materials

We use galvanized steel mesh, metal flashing, commercial caulk, and hardware cloth. No shortcuts. No spray foam over a hole and calling it done.

Step 4

Warranty

Every sealed entry point is covered by our warranty. If an animal gets back in through our work, we come back and fix it at no charge.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does your exclusion warranty cover? +

Our warranty covers every entry point we seal. If an animal gets back in through one of our sealed areas, we come back and fix it at no charge. The warranty length depends on the scope of work and is outlined in your service agreement.

What areas do you seal? +

We seal roof returns, soffit gaps, gable vents, ridge vents, plumbing penetrations, foundation cracks, dryer vents, chimney caps, and any other opening an animal could use to enter your home.

How long does exclusion work take? +

Most exclusion jobs take 1 to 3 days depending on the size of your home and the number of entry points. We'll give you a timeline during the inspection.

What materials do you use for sealing? +

We use professional-grade materials including galvanized steel mesh, metal flashing, commercial caulk, expanding foam, and hardware cloth. Everything is chosen for durability and to match your home's look.

What happens if animals get back in after exclusion? +

If animals re-enter through any area we sealed, we come back at no cost under our warranty. We also inspect to find out how they got in and make sure it doesn't happen again.
