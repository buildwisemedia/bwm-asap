That Smell Won't Go Away on Its Own

A terrible odor coming from your walls, attic, or crawlspace usually means an animal has died somewhere you can't reach. The smell gets worse before it gets better — and it can take months to fade without removal.

Unbearable Odor

The smell of a decomposing animal is one of the worst odors you'll experience. It spreads through your vents, permeates walls, and makes rooms unusable.

Health Hazard

Decomposing animals release bacteria and attract insects like flies and beetles. These secondary pests can spread throughout your home and create new problems.

Hard to Find

You can smell it but you can't find it. Dead animals end up in wall cavities, between floors, inside ductwork, and under crawlspaces. You need someone who knows where to look.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you find a dead animal in the wall?
We use a combination of experience, scent tracking, and thermal detection to locate deceased animals inside walls, ceilings, and other hard-to-reach spaces. We know where animals tend to get trapped and die, so we can often find them quickly without tearing up your home.
How long does the smell last?
Without removal, the smell from a dead animal can last anywhere from two weeks to several months depending on the size of the animal and the temperature. The sooner you call, the sooner we can remove the source and treat the odor so your home smells normal again.
Is a dead animal a health risk?
Yes, a dead animal can attract flies, maggots, and other pests. It can also release bacteria and pathogens as it decomposes. The odor alone can cause nausea and headaches. Professional removal and disinfection is the safest way to handle the situation.
Can you remove dead animals from hard-to-reach places?
Yes, we regularly remove dead animals from inside walls, under floors, in attic spaces, inside ductwork, and under decks. We have the tools and experience to reach difficult spots with minimal disruption to your home. We repair any access points we create.
