There's a Terrible Smell and You Can't Find It

It started a few days ago. A faint odor you couldn't place. Now it's getting worse. You've checked everywhere — under the sink, behind the fridge, in the garage. Nothing.

The smell is probably coming from a dead animal in your walls, attic, or crawl space. It happens more often than people think, especially after wildlife gets into your home.

If this sounds familiar, don't wait for it to go away on its own. The smell gets worse before it gets better, and it attracts flies and other pests.

How We Handle Dead Animal Removal

Step 1

Locate

We use years of experience and specialized tools to pinpoint the exact location. We check walls, attics, crawl spaces, ductwork, and other common spots.

Step 2

Safe Removal

We carefully remove the animal with proper protective equipment. If it's inside a wall, we make a small access point to reach it.

Step 3

Proper Disposal

The animal is bagged and disposed of following all local regulations. No mess left behind.

Step 4

Odor Elimination

We apply professional odor-neutralizing products to eliminate the smell at the source. No air fresheners or cover-ups — real odor elimination.

Step 5

Sanitize

We disinfect the area where the animal was found. This kills bacteria and prevents secondary pest problems like flies or maggots.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if there's a dead animal in my house? +

The most common sign is a strong, unpleasant smell that gets worse over several days. You may also notice an increase in flies. The smell is often strongest near walls, in the attic, or near vents.

How long does the smell from a dead animal last? +

Without removal, a dead animal smell can last 2 to 8 weeks depending on the size of the animal and the temperature. Removal and treatment eliminates the odor much faster.

How much does dead animal removal cost? +

Dead animal removal typically costs between $150 and $500. The cost depends on the location of the animal and how difficult it is to access. We provide a quote after locating the animal.

Can you remove a dead animal from under my house? +

Yes. We regularly remove dead animals from crawl spaces, under decks, and beneath homes. Our technicians are equipped to work in tight spaces safely.

Can you remove a dead animal from inside a wall? +

Yes. We use our experience and specialized tools to pinpoint the location inside the wall. We make a small access point, remove the animal, sanitize the area, and repair the opening.
