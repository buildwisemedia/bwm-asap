The Animals Are Gone — But the Mess Isn't

After wildlife leaves your attic or crawlspace, they leave behind droppings, urine, and nesting materials. This waste is more than just unpleasant — it's a real health risk for your family.

Raccoon Roundworm

Raccoon droppings contain roundworm eggs that can survive for years. If inhaled or accidentally ingested, they can cause serious illness — especially in children.

Bat Guano & Histoplasmosis

Bat droppings grow a fungus that causes histoplasmosis, a lung infection. Disturbing bat guano without proper protection can release dangerous spores into the air.

Rodent Hantavirus

Rodent droppings and urine can carry hantavirus, which causes a potentially fatal respiratory illness. Sweeping or vacuuming without precautions can spread the virus.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is animal waste dangerous?
Yes, animal waste is a serious health hazard. Raccoon droppings can carry roundworm eggs that are dangerous to humans. Bat guano can cause histoplasmosis, a lung infection. Rodent droppings and urine can spread hantavirus and other diseases. Professional cleanup is important.
What diseases can animal droppings cause?
Raccoon droppings can cause baylisascaris, a serious roundworm infection. Bat guano causes histoplasmosis, which affects the lungs. Rodent waste can spread hantavirus, leptospirosis, and salmonella. These risks make professional cleanup essential — don't try to clean it yourself.
Do you clean attic insulation?
Yes, if your attic insulation is contaminated with animal waste, we can remove it. Contaminated insulation holds odor, bacteria, and parasites. We remove the affected insulation, disinfect the area, and can coordinate replacement insulation if needed.
How long does disinfection take?
Most disinfection jobs take one to two days depending on the size of the contaminated area. Large attic cleanouts with insulation removal may take longer. We give you a clear timeline during the inspection so you know exactly what to expect.
