Attic Cleanup and Disinfection

Remove droppings, nesting materials, and health hazards. Protect your family.

The Animals Are Gone. The Mess Is Still There.

The trapping is done. The holes are sealed. But your attic is full of droppings, urine-soaked insulation, and nesting debris. It smells. And it's not just unpleasant — it's a health risk.

Raccoon droppings can carry roundworm. Bat guano can cause histoplasmosis, a serious lung infection. Rodent waste can carry hantavirus. These are real risks, not scare tactics.

If this sounds familiar, you need professional cleanup — not a broom and a mask from the hardware store.

How We Handle Cleanup and Disinfection

Step 1

Assess Contamination

We inspect the affected area and determine the level of contamination. We check for droppings, urine staining, nesting materials, and insulation damage.

Step 2

Remove Waste

We remove all droppings, contaminated insulation, nesting materials, and debris. Everything is bagged and disposed of properly. No shortcuts.

Step 3

Disinfect

We apply commercial-grade antimicrobial products to all affected surfaces. This kills bacteria, neutralizes odors, and eliminates health hazards.

Step 4

Restore Insulation

If insulation is damaged or contaminated beyond cleaning, we remove it and install new insulation. Your attic goes back to the way it should be.

Frequently Asked Questions

What health risks do animal droppings pose? +

Animal droppings can carry serious health risks. Raccoon droppings may contain roundworm eggs. Bat guano can cause histoplasmosis, a lung infection. Rodent droppings can carry hantavirus. Professional cleanup protects your family from these hazards.

How long does attic cleanup take? +

Most cleanup jobs take 1 to 2 days depending on the size of the affected area and the level of contamination. Large jobs that require insulation replacement may take longer.

What exactly gets cleaned? +

We remove all droppings, urine-soaked insulation, nesting materials, and debris. We then disinfect all surfaces with commercial-grade antimicrobial products. If insulation is damaged, we can replace it.

Will the smell go away after cleanup? +

Yes. Odors from animal waste are caused by bacteria. Once we remove the contaminated materials and disinfect the area, the smell goes away. We also use odor-neutralizing products for stubborn cases.

Does insurance cover attic cleanup? +

Some homeowner's insurance policies cover animal damage cleanup. We recommend contacting your insurance company to check your coverage. We can provide documentation and photos to support your claim.
