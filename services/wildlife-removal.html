Hearing Noises in Your Attic?

If something is scratching, thumping, or running around in your walls or attic, you have a wildlife problem. And it won't go away on its own.

Property Damage

Animals chew through wires, tear up insulation, and damage ductwork. A raccoon in your attic can cause thousands of dollars in damage if left alone.

Health Risks

Animal droppings carry diseases like raccoon roundworm, histoplasmosis, and leptospirosis. The longer they stay, the worse the contamination gets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What animals do you remove?
We remove raccoons, squirrels, bats, snakes, opossums, armadillos, and other wildlife common to Metro Atlanta. Every animal is handled humanely and relocated safely away from your property. We follow all Georgia DNR guidelines for wildlife handling.
Is wildlife removal humane?
Yes, all of our wildlife removal methods are humane. We use live traps, one-way exclusion doors, and hands-on removal techniques. Animals are relocated to approved habitats far from your home. We never use poisons or lethal traps for wildlife.
How long does wildlife removal take?
Most wildlife removal jobs take between 3 to 7 days from start to finish. The timeline depends on the species and how many animals are present. We monitor traps daily and keep you updated throughout the entire process.
Will the animals come back?
Not if we seal the entry points. After removing the animals, we find every gap, crack, and opening they used to get in. We seal those with professional-grade materials so new animals cannot enter. This is why exclusion is a critical step in the process.
Do you remove bats?
Yes, we handle bat removal using exclusion methods approved by the Georgia DNR. We install one-way bat valves that let bats leave but prevent them from re-entering. Bat removal timing may depend on maternity season regulations in Georgia.
